## The 5 Design Mistakes That Drive Customers Away

### 1. Poor Quality Photos (or Poorly Displayed)

A traveler consulting your hotel before booking simultaneously compares your site with Booking.com, which invests heavily in photo display. If your images take more than a second to load or display in 4:3 on mobile ‚Äî you've already lost.

**What Hotels That Convert Do:**
- Photos in optimized WebP format (80% lighter than JPEG)
- Progressive lazy loading with blurred placeholder
- Immersive full-screen gallery with smooth transitions

### 2. Abandoned Booking Funnel Halfway

On average, **68% of users abandon the booking process** before completing. Main causes: too many steps, non-mobile-optimized forms, no visible SSL security indication.

Hotels working with specialized agencies reduce this abandonment rate to **35-40%** with a simplified funnel in 3 steps maximum.

### 3. A Site Not Designed for Mobile

In 2025, **62% of hotel searches** start on mobile. Yet, most upscale hotel websites were designed desktop-first with approximate mobile adaptation. Result: a "Book" button too small, an unreadable calendar, confusing navigation.

A **mobile-first** site isn't a luxury ‚Äî it's the basic condition for existence.

### 4. A Visual Identity That Tells Nothing

Luxury hotels sell an emotion before a bed. Your site must transmit that emotion in the first 3 seconds: typography, colors, subtle scroll animation, editorial tone ‚Äî everything must resonate with your positioning.

A Wix template or a ‚Ç¨59 WordPress theme can't do that.

### 5. Absence of Visible Social Proof

**89% of travelers** consult reviews before booking. If your TripAdvisor or Google reviews aren't natively integrated into the booking journey ‚Äî next to the "Book" button, on the room page, at the bottom of the page ‚Äî you're leaving trust on the table.
